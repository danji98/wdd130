Maracaibo

Maracaibo is a tropical city located in the east of Venezuela, just next to Colombia. It is known for its consistently warm climate, making it one of the hottest cities in the world, reaching temperatures of almost 50 C°.

One of the city's most remarkable natural attractions is the "Catatumbo lightning," a unique weather phenomenon where lightning strikes occur almost nightly, creating a spectacular display in the night sky.

Economically, Maracaibo plays a significant role in Venezuela, primarily due to its strategic location as a major port on Lake Maracaibo.

The city is a hub for oil-related industries, with several oil refineries and petrochemical plants in the area. This makes it a vital contributor to Venezuela's oil production and export.
